What is Fast Fashion?


Fast fashion is a business model that involves producing low-priced, trendy clothing quickly to meet current trends. Usually these clothes are poorly made with a short term lasting due to its material. There are various negatives when it comes to fast fashion, including its extensive damage to the planet, exploits workers, and harms animals!

Examples of brands:

There are many brands which do fast fashion. To exemplify this, a prominent fast fashion brand is Temu or Shein. Beyond this, others include H&M, ZARA, Urban Outfitters, Brandy Melville, etc.

  • 1. Shein

    • Shein is a fast fashion brand that begain in 2008 but it wasn't well known until 2020 due to the quarintine. Due to it's cheap prices and keeping up with the latest trends, everyone shops there.

  • 2. H&M

    • H&M began in 1947 and it became popular due to their cheap prices and stylish clothes that can be accesable to anyone. At the same time it's considered fast-fashion because of the use of child labor, unpaid workers, etc. They've also been doing bad for a while now.

  • 3. Forever 21

    • Forever 21 began in 1984 and it became popular as similar to the two other brands, or any fast fashion brand - they all are based on making clothes to meet their customers needs and being cheap. Similar to the other two brands they also rely a lot on child labor, forcing long hours with no pay, etc.
